## 1. Tournament format — VERIFIED

- **48 teams**, first World Cup at this size; co-hosted by USA, Mexico, Canada; **104 matches** total (up from 64).
  Source: [FIFA — How the FIFA World Cup 26 will work with 48 teams](https://www.fifa.com/en/articles/article-fifa-world-cup-2026-mexico-canada-usa-new-format-tournament-football-soccer), [Wikipedia — 2026 FIFA World Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup)
- **12 groups (A–L) of 4 teams**; each team plays 3 group matches.
- **Advancement: top 2 of each group (24) + 8 best third-placed teams = 32** → new **Round of 32**, then single-elimination R16, QF, SF, third-place match, Final.
- Winner plays **8 matches** total. Final: **July 19, 2026, MetLife Stadium** (New York/New Jersey).
  Source: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup), [FIFA tournament hub](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026)

## 2. Group-stage tiebreakers (2026 edition, exact order) — VERIFIED

**Major change from 2022: head-to-head criteria now come BEFORE overall goal difference**, and the **FIFA World Ranking replaces drawing of lots** as the final tiebreaker.
Sources: [MLSSoccer.com — 2026 FIFA World Cup tiebreakers](https://www.mlssoccer.com/news/2026-fifa-world-cup-tiebreakers-how-can-teams-advance), [FIFA — groups, qualification rules & tie-breakers](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/groups-how-teams-qualify-tie-breakers), [FOX Sports — group stage tiebreakers](https://www.foxsports.com/stories/soccer/fifa-world-cup-group-stage-third-place-tiebreakers), corroborated by [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup) ("The first tiebreaker is now the number of points earned in head-to-head matches between the tied teams").

Teams level on **points** in a group are ranked by, in order:

1. Points obtained in group matches **between the tied teams** (head-to-head points)
2. Goal difference in matches **between the tied teams**
3. Goals scored in matches **between the tied teams**
4. Goal difference in **all** group matches
5. Goals scored in **all** group matches
6. **Team conduct score** (fair play: yellow/red cards, players and team officials)
7. **FIFA/Coca-Cola Men's World Ranking** (most recent edition published before the finals)

> Note: **drawing of lots is NOT used in 2026** — sources consistently state FIFA ranking is the final criterion. (UNVERIFIED nuance: whether the raw regulations retain lots as a theoretical last resort behind the ranking; the primary regulations PDF was not directly fetched.)

### Ranking of third-placed teams (for the 8 best-thirds) — VERIFIED

Same MLSSoccer source: 1. Points → 2. Goal difference → 3. Goals scored → 4. Team conduct score → 5. FIFA World Ranking.

## 3. Knockout rules — VERIFIED

- Level after 90 minutes → **30 minutes of extra time**; still level → **penalty shootout**. No away goals, no golden goal, no straight-to-penalties round.
  Source: [Wikipedia — 2026 FIFA World Cup knockout stage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_knockout_stage) ("If the score of a match is level at the end of 90 minutes of playing time, 30 minutes of extra time will be played. If the scores are still tied, the match will be decided by a penalty shootout.")
- A **third-place match** is played (same source).

## Quick-reference for the simulation engine

- 48 teams → 12×4 groups → top-2 + 8 best thirds → R32 (fixed template above, Annex C third-slotting) → R16 → QF → SF → 3rd-place → Final.
- Tiebreaks: **pts → H2H pts → H2H GD → H2H GF → overall GD → overall GF → conduct score → FIFA ranking** (no lots).
- Best-thirds ranking: pts → GD → GF → conduct → FIFA ranking.
- Knockouts: 90' → ET 30' → penalties; third-place match exists.
- Altitude flags: Azteca (Mexico City) 2,240 m; Akron (Guadalajara) 1,566 m; everything else < 600 m.
