## 1. Hosts and automatic qualification

### Main hosts — VERIFIED
- The 2030 World Cup will be jointly hosted by **Morocco, Portugal and Spain**. Confirmed by the extraordinary FIFA Congress on **11 December 2024** (decision by acclamation).
  - https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/articles/world-cup-2030-spain-portugal-morocco-host-centenary-argentina-uruguay-paraguay
  - https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2030_FIFA_World_Cup
  - https://inside.fifa.com/tournament-organisation/world-cup-2030
- First World Cup spanning more than one confederation (UEFA + CAF), first in Africa since 2010, first ever in North Africa.
  - https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2030_FIFA_World_Cup

### Centenary arrangement — VERIFIED
- To mark 100 years since Uruguay 1930, **three centenary celebration matches** will be played in South America: one at **Estadio Centenario, Montevideo (Uruguay)** — the 1930 final venue — plus one each at **Estadio Monumental, Buenos Aires (Argentina)** and **Estadio Osvaldo Domínguez Dibb (Defensores del Chaco), Asunción (Paraguay)**. The rest of the tournament is in Morocco/Portugal/Spain.
  - https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2030_FIFA_World_Cup
  - https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/articles/world-cup-2030-spain-portugal-morocco-host-centenary-argentina-uruguay-paraguay
  - https://www.foxsports.com/stories/soccer/2030-world-cup-locations-hosts-dates-100-years
- The multi-continent travel makes it the longest World Cup window (~44 days vs 39 in 2026). — VERIFIED (single detailed source; treat exact day count as soft)
  - https://www.foxsports.com/stories/soccer/2030-world-cup-locations-hosts-dates-100-years

### Automatic qualifiers — VERIFIED: ALL SIX
- **All six nations qualify automatically**: Morocco, Portugal, Spain (main hosts) **and** Argentina, Paraguay, Uruguay (centenary hosts). This is the most host berths in World Cup history.
  - https://www.batimes.com.ar/news/sports/argentina-paraguay-and-uruguay-get-automatic-places-for-2030-world-cup.phtml
  - https://www.worldfootball.net/news/ne5702453/trio-secures-automatic-places-for-2030-world-cup/
  - https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/articles/world-cup-2030-hosts-qualified-bidding-spain-argentina-morocco-uruguay-portugal-paraguay
  - https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2030_FIFA_World_Cup ("All six host nations qualified for the World Cup.")
- **UNVERIFIED**: whether the three CONMEBOL host slots and two UEFA host slots (and Morocco's CAF slot) are deducted from their confederations' quotas or granted on top. FIFA has not published the 2030 allocation (see §3). Simulation should treat this as a parameter.

## 2. Tournament format

- **48 teams** — VERIFIED as the working assumption and widely reported plan (same as 2026); FIFA's own 2030 pages and press coverage describe 48 teams / 42 remaining berths. However, FIFA's format regulations for 2030 are not formally published; Wikipedia still lists teams "TBA".
  - https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2030_FIFA_World_Cup
  - https://worldcupranking.com/world-cup-2030-qualifiers/
- **Group structure — UNVERIFIED for 2030** (not yet formally confirmed). The expected template is the **2026 format: 12 groups of 4; top two per group plus the 8 best third-placed teams advance to a round of 32; 104 matches**.
  - 2026 format (VERIFIED): https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup
- **64-team proposal — proposed, NOT adopted (status as of mid-2026)**:
  - CONMEBOL proposed a one-off 64-team centenary expansion at the March 2025 FIFA Council meeting; discussed again with FIFA president Infantino later in 2025.
  - No FIFA Council decision has been made; reporting indicates FIFA is "not considering" it / it would not pass Council; UEFA (Čeferin) and CONCACAF (Montagliani) publicly opposed.
  - https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/world-cup-with-64-teams-fifa-not-considering-expanding-2030-event-further-despite-proposal-per-report/
  - https://www.foxsports.com/articles/soccer/fifa-reportedly-unlikely-to-expand-to-a-64-team-2030-world-cup-despite-recent-proposals-from-conmebol

## 4. Confederation qualifying formats for 2030

Status as of 2026-07-08 — mostly NOT finalized; two confederations have announced plans:

- **CONCACAF — ANNOUNCED (VERIFIED, 6 Feb 2026)**: four-stage path — Round 1 (Sep 2027, teams ranked 14–35, home-and-away); Round 2 (Oct–Nov 2027 + Mar 2028, 24 teams in six groups); Final round (Jun 2028 → Sep–Oct 2029, 12 teams in three groups); playoff (Nov 2029) among best third-placed teams for the intercontinental playoff berth. Slot count still subject to FIFA confirmation.
  - https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/47851345/concacaf-2030-world-cup-qualifying-usa-mexico-canada-fifa
- **UEFA — PLAN ANNOUNCED (VERIFIED as an announced plan, 20 May 2026; not yet a finalized/ratified format)**: Champions League/Nations League-style two-tier system. League 1: top 36 nations (seeded by 2028–29 Nations League) in three 12-team leagues, each team playing 6 matches vs 6 different opponents; best-ranked teams qualify directly, remaining berths via playoffs also involving League 2 (remaining ~18 nations, three groups). Nations League to be restructured (three leagues of 18) from 2028–29.
  - https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/48826270/uefa-announces-plans-champions-league-style-qualification-world-cup

## 5. Final draw template: 2026 draw rules — VERIFIED

Held 5 December 2025, Kennedy Center, Washington, D.C. Rules (use as the 2030 template until FIFA publishes 2030 procedures):

- **Four pots of 12**, by FIFA Men's World Ranking.
- **Pot 1**: the three hosts (Mexico, Canada, USA) seeded into Pot 1 + the 9 highest-ranked qualified teams. Pot 2: next 12 (ranks 13–24 among qualified). Pot 3: next 12. Pot 4: lowest 6 + 6 placeholders (4 UEFA playoff winners, 2 inter-confederation playoff winners).
- **Confederation constraint**: max **1 team per confederation per group**, except **UEFA: at least 1 and at most 2 per group**.
- **Top-seed separation**: ranked #1 (Spain) and #2 (Argentina) placed in opposite bracket pathways; likewise #3 (France) and #4 (England) — so the top four seeds can only meet from the semifinals if they win their groups.
- One team drawn from each pot per group; 12 groups (A–L).
